# Comparison of BOM vs Routing

BOM (Bill of Materials) and Routing are both related to manufacturing, but they serve different purposes. Here's a comparison:

## BOM (Bill of Materials)

- A list of all raw materials, components, and assemblies required to manufacture a product
- Specifies the quantities of each item needed
- Does not define the sequence of operations or workstations
- Used for material planning, procurement, and cost calculation

## Routing

- Defines the sequence of operations (steps) in the manufacturing process
- Specifies the workstations where each operation is performed
- Includes time estimates for each operation
- Used for production scheduling, capacity planning, and workflow management

## Key Differences

| Aspect | BOM | Routing |
|--------|-----|---------|
| Purpose | Material list | Process sequence |
| Focus | What materials | How operations |
| Timing | Material requirements | Production scheduling |
| Cost | Material costs | Labor/overhead costs |

## When to Use

- Use **BOM** when you need to know what materials go into a product
- Use **Routing** when you need to plan how the product will be manufactured
- Both are often used together in a complete manufacturing system
